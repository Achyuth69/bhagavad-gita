The Bhagavad Gītā

The Bhagavad Gītā, often called the “Song of the Divine,” is one of the most influential spiritual texts in the history of humanity. Composed over two millennia ago as part of the epic Mahābhārata, the Gītā presents a profound dialogue between Lord Krishna and the warrior Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.

18 Chapters

The scripture is divided into eighteen chapters, each presenting a different path of spiritual realization such as Karma Yoga (action), Bhakti Yoga (devotion), and Jñāna Yoga (knowledge).

700 Verses

Across its 700 verses, the Gītā explores the nature of duty, the soul, righteousness, and the purpose of life, guiding humanity toward wisdom and inner balance.

Universal Wisdom

The teachings of the Bhagavad Gītā transcend religion and time. Its philosophy has inspired scholars, leaders, and seekers across centuries.
